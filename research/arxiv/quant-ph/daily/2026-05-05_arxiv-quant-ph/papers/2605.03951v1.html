Factoring 2048 bit RSA integers with a half-million-qubit modular atomic processor

原摘要

Shor's algorithm is one of the most promising applications of quantum computers. However, since ~10^6 physical qubits are believed to be required for established approaches, the algorithm will need to be distributed across many modules. In this paper, we provide a distributed compilation of Shor's algorithm for a modular atomic processor architecture with ~5×10^5 qubits. We show that by using a modular architecture with nearest-neighbor connectivity, we can factor 2048-bit RSA integers with a quantum circuit depth of O(n^3) and a total gate count that is competitive with monolithic superconducting approaches. Our analysis includes detailed resource estimates, error correction overhead, and communication costs between modules. We also discuss the practical challenges in building such a system, including the need for high-fidelity inter-module connections and the effects of crosstalk between modules. Our results suggest that modular atomic processors could provide a viable path to scalable quantum computing for cryptographic applications.
